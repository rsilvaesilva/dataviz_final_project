# Visualization 1: Religious Service Attendance

attendance <- data %>%
  filter(Category %in% c(
    "Weekly",
    "Nearly Weekly/Monthly",
    "Less often"
  ))

attendance_long <- attendance %>%
  pivot_longer(
    cols = all_of(belief_cols),
    names_to = "Belief",
    values_to = "Percent"
  )

ggplot(attendance_long,
       aes(x = Category,
           y = Percent,
           fill = Belief)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity",
           position = "dodge") +
  labs(
    title = "Beliefs by Religious Service Attendance",
    x = "Attendance Frequency",
    y = "Percent"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

This is to be expected those that tend to go more often to their religious services be it mass or congregation, they are more likely to hold beliefs in line with their religion and they arent “lukewarm” believers, but the difference between those who go weekly and monthly is not much which is surprising but then again in today’s day and age there is seldom much time to go or the energy to go sadly. # Visualization 8:Heatmap (All Groups)

heat_data <- data %>%
  pivot_longer(
    cols = all_of(belief_cols),
    names_to = "Belief",
    values_to = "Percent"
  )

ggplot(heat_data,
       aes(x = Belief,
           y = Category,
           fill = Percent)) +
  geom_tile() +
  scale_fill_gradient(
    low = "red",
    high = "darkblue"
  ) +
  labs(
    title = "Religious Beliefs Across Demographic Groups",
    x = "Belief Type",
    y = "Demographic Group"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

Here we can get a better comparisson to each category one next to the other, here we can really tell that those that do not believe in any religion really are less likely to believe anything meanwhile overall there is a great variance between age groups, attendance, college grads and household income.
